PROGRAM CARD 1: MAMA AID
Card Eyebrow (Small text above headline):
DIGITAL HEALTH NAVIGATION

Card Headline:
Mama Aid
Card Tagline:
Every mama deserves expert care
Card Description (Full):
Nigeria: 512 maternal deaths per 100,000 births. UK: 7 per 100,000. The gap isn't knowledge—it's access.

Mama Aid is our answer. Through AI-powered WhatsApp navigation, we connect diaspora nurses with expectant mothers across Africa. One nurse can now support 50-100 mothers instead of 5-10, because intelligent technology handles routine questions while humans focus on urgent cases and emotional support.

The result? Free, expert healthcare guidance for mothers who need it most. Life-saving triage that escalates emergencies instantly. And diaspora nurses finally able to give back to their home communities at scale.

This is healthcare knowledge exchange powered by technology, driven by compassion, and scaled for impact.

• £4.50 per mother/month (vs £50+ manual care)

PROGRAM CARD 2: NHA ACADEMY
Card Eyebrow:
PROFESSIONAL EDUCATION

Card Headline:
NHA Academy
Card Tagline:
Build the career you've always wanted
Card Description (Full):
You didn't become a nurse to stay stagnant. You became a nurse to grow, to specialize, to build a career that matters. NHA Academy is where that transformation happens.

We offer world-class training across four pillars: Medical Aesthetics (Level 7 certification with melanin-rich skin specialization), Clinical Skills (advanced procedures and evidence-based practice), Business & Entrepreneurship (from employee to practice owner), and Leadership & Management (including digital health navigator training).

What makes us different? Our hybrid model. Learn theory online at your own pace—evenings, weekends, whenever works for you. Then come together for intensive hands-on practice with expert supervision. You arrive prepared, so practical training focuses on doing, not lecturing.

The result? Better outcomes, lower costs, and qualifications that actually prepare you for practice. This isn't just professional development—it's career transformation.

Page Title:
About Nurse Hub Africa
OUR STORY SECTION
Section Headline:
From Lagos to London to Leading Change
Full Story Copy:
Nurse Hub Africa was born from a simple observation that led to a powerful question.

The observation: Over 100,000 African nurses work abroad—in the UK, USA, Canada, Australia, and across Europe. These aren't just any nurses. They're among the best-trained healthcare professionals in the world, having navigated rigorous international licensing, practiced in state-of-the-art facilities, and developed expertise that would be invaluable back home.

The question: What if this diaspora expertise could flow back to African communities in meaningful, sustainable ways?

Founded in 2017 in Nigeria, we started as a community of nurses supporting each other through the challenges of migration, licensing, and practice abroad. We shared job opportunities, visa tips, and encouragement. We celebrated each other's wins and supported each other through the tough moments.

In 2019, we took it further. We hosted the Pan-African Healthcare Awards, bringing nurses together from across the continent to celebrate excellence and innovation. It was powerful. It was necessary. And it showed us something important: when African nurses connect, extraordinary things happen.

But we knew we could do more. What if connection led to impact? What if our network became a force for transforming healthcare systems?

In 2026, we restructured as a UK Community Interest Company—a social enterprise designed to generate sustainable revenue while ensuring every pound serves our community mission. We launched four flagship programs, each designed to promote healthcare knowledge exchange in innovative ways.

Today, we serve over 100,000 African nurses across 54 countries and the diaspora. We're not just a network anymore. We're a movement. We're proving that diaspora doesn't mean disconnect—it means expanded capacity. We're showing that African nursing expertise, when properly connected and supported, can transform healthcare across an entire continent.

And we're just getting started.
